Delivery Partner Retention

Delivery Partner Loyalty Program

In the fiercely competitive landscape of the logistics industry, businesses are grappling with the challenge of retaining their delivery partners. With escalating competition and soaring attrition rates which goes as high as 50%, the need to address this issue has become critical.
This case study dives into the heart of the problem, aiming to provide effective solutions that will significantly boost long-term retention rates for organizations in the logistics sector. For this case study, I have specifically picked Swiggy to base my research and design on.

Business goal

Move the needle towards long term retention.

Based on research, I have identified the main factor affecting long term commitment to Swiggy as a sentiment of not being fairly rewarded. An approach is a tier-based rewards program. All participating partners automatically qualify for tier status and rewards. By earning points and maintaining a certain level of quality, they have the ability to unlock each tier status and rewards. We want to give drivers an alternative outlet to be rewarded.

Key Insights
A sentiment of not being fairly rewarded was the outlier for absence of long term commitment.

↗ We want to give drivers an alternative outlet to be rewarded.

Overview

Reward marketplace can be further divided into three problems
Problem 1

How many points do I have?

FINAL DESIGN - pOints hub
Points hub is designed for partners to audit their progress towards status and can be evolved to be a space for auditing all mechanisms for earning rewards. It is an entry to lifetime points
Problem 2

What rewards can I get?

FINAL DESIGN - Rewards marketplace
This approach prioritizes attainability of rewards and creates space for motivation. Horizontal scroll creates sense of mystery and excitement for top rewards. This system is scalable for a rewards list that is likely to grow over time.
Problem 2.5

How can I purchase this reward?

FINAL DESIGN - Status and lifetime points reward
A single catch all page for two ways to get this reward is much better than a status rewards details page that focuses on communicating how to unlock the reward.
Ideally, there’d be no repetition between rewards unlocked with status and rewards purchased with points. However, this design was done for the likely chance that product needs will require this to happen.
Problem 3

What rewards do I have?

FINAL DESIGN -Rewards shelf
Instead of slotting rewards below status progression, all rewards can be grouped together under another tab.

Summary

Final design solutions for the three problem statements
